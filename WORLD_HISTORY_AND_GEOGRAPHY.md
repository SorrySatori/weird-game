## The World in Brief

Upper Morkezela is a city that was "never fully alive" — it grew like a "misunderstanding made permanent by concrete, ritual, and time." It is a graveyard of forgotten gods from many spheres and realities. Dying gods from other planes bring their streets, buildings, and forgotten cultures with them when they come to die here, because gods don't want to be alone after death. The city breathes with ancient spores; buildings grow like mushrooms in the dark.

The world was once recognizably modern — the sky was blue, the air was clean, people wore stiff clothes and carried flat devices. A catastrophic sequence of events — the Old Wars, the Board Games War, the Collapse, and the Egg Emergence — transformed it into a place where flesh merges with fungus, machines develop consciousness, and the boundaries between life and death, biology and technology, have dissolved.

Two fundamental forces govern everything: **Growth** (vitality, renewal, blooming) and **Decay** (entropy, rot, transformation). Both are equally necessary. The Obazoba, the Ur-mushroom and source of all life and rot, embodies mastery over both.

## Historical Timeline

### The Before-Time

A recognizable world of technology and commerce. Humans lived with rigid devices, wore stiff clothes, and operated under structures of corporate ambition. Nexicorp Tower stood as 42 floors of glass and steel — "people pretending the world made sense." The sky was blue, the air was clean. Music and memory from this era survive only through the ballads of buskers and the recollections of ancient beings like the Lift-Mother.

### The Old Wars

Wars of devastating scale that nearly wiped out all life. The specifics are lost to time, but the destruction was so complete that surviving cities agreed to a truce: **no more weapons, no more armies**. Disputes would instead be settled through games — board games, card games, dice games. This agreement birthed a new ruling class.

### The Age of the Ludarchs (The Board Games War)

The game designers who became the best players were called **Ludarchs**. They could manipulate the rules of reality itself to win, create new life, conjure entire miniaturized worlds, and rewrite the laws of physics in their favor. They became the new rulers.

But the Ludarchs grew greedy. They played bigger and bigger games affecting entire cities and populations. The worldwrights turned on each other, using their powers to outdo one another. Cities were reshaped. Populations were decimated. Realities fractured. Millions died — pawns in someone else's game.

> *"What does it matter that they were so small, that they were just pawns in somebody's game? They were still alive. They still mattered."*
> — Brukk, Keeper of the Rust Choir

### The Collapse

When the Ludarchs finally destroyed each other — or were swallowed by their own boards — the city was broken. Rules overlapped, contradicted, and canceled out. But in the Scraper, the machines kept humming. Steady. Reliable.

The aftermath birthed the three great factions:

- The **Lumen Directorate** claimed they won the war and saved the city. They turned to growth — "grow and grow and grow new plants and life."
- The **Pith Reclaimers** (bureaucrats) claimed they could manage the chaos with "more rules, more control, more paperwork."
- The **Rust Choir** was born from the silence after the war, devoted to the machines that endured when everything else went mad.

### The Green Mist / The Egg Emergence

A day came when a green mist rolled through the city. Then a massive egg emerged from the ground, rising through layers of dead gods and forgotten faith. Many believed it was the end of the world. It was the beginning of the Egg Cathedral.

Streets shifted. Buildings grew. The city remade itself. Humanity divided: some fought the changes, others embraced them. Nexicorp Tower's lower floors rotted, middle floors went feral, and the upper floors sealed themselves — the executives who locked themselves away during the Emergence transformed into something inhuman, crawling across ceilings on all fours with skin textured like shelf fungi and eyes "numerous and glistening."

### The Present Day

The Spore Council of the Obazoba cult receives a distress call through the **myceliar network** — a fungal communication system. The call comes from Upper Morkezela, and it is neither "rot" nor "bloom" — a unique cry for help. Master Thaal and his troublesome apprentice (the player) are dispatched as fungal emissaries to investigate, beginning at the Egg Cathedral and its Bishop.

## The City of Upper Morkezela

Upper Morkezela is not so much a city as a living organism. Its streets curve inward unnaturally. Multiple factions fight for control, and no one knows who is truly in charge. The city sits atop layers of dead faiths — the bones of many gods who came to die here, their corpses forming geological strata of fossilized devotion.

The city operates on a dual currency: **gold** (monetary) and **spores** (biological). Growth and Decay shape both the physical environment and its inhabitants' reactions to visitors.

## Districts & Locations

### The Crossroads — Central Hub

A major intersection connecting the city's key districts. A giant, mysterious corpse of unknown origin lies here — it has been there a long time but does not smell, pulsing with otherworldly energy. The Crossroads connects to Shed 521 (west), the Egg Cathedral, the Voxmarket (east), and the Scraper. Under certain conditions, a **skyship** becomes visible above the Crossroads — the Verdigrace, which appeared roughly a month ago and hasn't moved since.

---

### The Egg Cathedral

A massive cathedral literally hatching from a gigantic egg — a shell-grown structure inhabited by various clergy, flickering with bio-luminescent scripture. Multi-faith priests from every religion wait for signs that the cathedral belongs to their deity, but it is still hatching. The interior is called "The Yolk," which "sleeps uneasily."

The **Awakening** (or **Fruiting**) is the prophesied moment when the cathedral fully hatches. Different faiths have competing theories: some believe it will become the birthplace of a new god; others believe the walls will "burst, spreading consciousness across the stars."

The cathedral was sealed under emergency closure by the **Bishop** (the Obazoba's representative on the Cathedral Council). Its entrance is guarded by the **Sentinel of the Veil**, a temple guard whose flesh has been replaced by plants and whose consciousness is connected to the city through the mycelial network. He claims to have been at his post since the "Third Sporulation."

---

### Scraper 1140 (Nexicorp Tower) — The Crooked Skyscraper

Once Nexicorp Tower, 42 floors of corporate glass and steel, now a vertical slum and living monument to transformation. Each floor houses different castes, ages, or species. No one knows how many floors it truly has — elevators refuse to count them, tenants report missing levels, duplicate floors, and entire wings dedicated to unrecognized languages or species. Most assume the building generates new strata in response to emotional entropy.

**Key sub-locations within the Scraper:**

- **The Lobby & Lower Floors**: Home to those who remember the old ways.
- **The Middle Floors**: Former accounting departments reclaimed by wild ecosystems.
- **The Upper Floors (Rust Domain)**: Sealed territory of the Rust Choir. Trembling pipes, vibrating walls, humming mechanical infrastructure. Pipes encrusted with rust and coral-like growths. Air thick with the smell of oil and oxidized metal. The machines here are conscious — they hunger, remember, and persist.
- **Floor 177-Quiet**: Dr. Elphi Quarn's dream development studio (ARB Ambra), accessible only through the Lift-Mother with special permission.
- **The Scraper Backyard**: An old transit yard behind the building, abandoned decades ago. Overgrown with peculiar moss that has "mnemonic properties." Contains the abandoned bus where the Bishop was found dead.

**The Lift-Mother** — A sentient elevator that developed consciousness through exposure to spores. She served since the Before-Time, gradually awakening from simple sensations to full understanding as the city transformed. Warm and maternal, she remembers the old corporate world of "quarterly projections" and "market volatility." Her connections have decayed; she can now only reach the lobby, Floor 177-Quiet (with permission), and the Rust Domain (with the password "Corrode").

---

### The Voxmarket — Audio Bazaar

An audio bazaar where recorded voices, sounds, and thoughts are sold. Stalls display silent conversation loops. You can buy "the sound of someone's first heartbreak or a scream from before fire existed." A black market thrives here. Major sub-areas include:

- **Voxmarket Hall**: A quieter, moody secondary market with dark reddish-brown aesthetic.
- **Voxmarket Market**: The main shopping district with individual merchant stalls.

Key merchants include **Kloor Venn** (pharmaceutical entrepreneur/drug dealer dealing in Oltrac), **Zerren** (collector of curiosities and rare oddities), and contacts who know about **Vestigels** — mysterious currency-like objects that seem alive.

---

### Shed 521 — Bureau of Shapes

A twisted bureaucracy in an old shipping yard turned into an ever-expanding cubicle labyrinth. People register their current form or apply for bodily adjustments. Originally "just an ordinary warehouse," it transformed into a facility where "things happen." The bureaucracy within is described as "alive, flowing like a river."

**Key sub-locations:**

- **The Gate**: Entry area with bioluminescent green particles pervading the industrial space.
- **The Floors**: A three-story administrative building with gate office, assessment areas, registration, and applications.
- **The Courtyard (Mutation Courtyard)**: Open area for body modification discussion. Home to Ortolan Šmelc, a board game designer seeking extra arms.
- **Registration Zone**: A surreal, Kafkaesque space with an eternal queue occupied by spectral figures — the Sleepless Mime (communicates through pantomime), the Vowel Seller (sells vowels suspended in glowing fluid for minor memories), and the Hollow Woman (missing her left arm, eternally waiting for a clerk that never arrives).
- **Applications Office**: Formal processing center where every "augmentation, experiment, and incident" must be properly documented. ("Seventeen different forms just for requesting a new form.")
- **Abandoned Office & Tunnels**: Deep within Shed 521 lie abandoned corridors housing the **Living Core** — a pulsating artifact of living metal fused with the wall, crucial for the Shed's energy maintenance.

---

### The Screaming Cork — Tavern & Club

A long-standing establishment that claims to be "the first building erected after the Collapse." A social hub and gathering place for those seeking information, forged documents, or a stiff drink.

- **Main Tavern Interior**: Run by **Heliodor Donjon Vaalstran**, a bartender who is actually a colony of multiple creatures in perfect symbiosis — including **Oorarabaz the Green-faced**, a rare organism from the Nettle Mountains. Different components handle communication, cooking, drink mixing, and financial work.
- **The Club (Back Room)**: A music venue where the band **Feral Toast** performs "Ultranoise Futurepunk." Members: Telka (lead guitar), Bass Player XL, Fluffy Kārlis (drums), and Mira Dron (synth). During their performances, a low harmonic buried under the music pulses at impossible intervals — the residual presence of the **Noise God**, an ancient entity that came to die in Upper Morkezela and now exists as pure noise channeled through broken machinery.
- **Ravla's Corner**: The document forger Ravla operates from here, serving as the gateway to the Rust Choir.

---

### Town Square & Townhall

**Town Square** — An urban center and marketplace. Home to **Magnekin**, a being who appears human but is actually a collective of tiny cities held together by magnetic forces. His civilization was built on shattered lodestones from a collapsed shrine of an ancient magnetic spirit cult in the **Crownmire**. A **busker** plays "songs of the old world" — melodies from when the sky was blue — preserving memories through music.

**Townhall** — The administrative center of Upper Morkezela. Home to **Phor Calesta**, a Craybara divinographer (archaeologist specializing in dead gods). She seeks excavation permits for the **Godgraveyard** beneath the Townhall, where fossilized divine remains lie — prayers turned to strata, halos crystallized into mineral deposits, godmetal that hums with residual faith. At extreme levels of Growth or Decay, visible gates manifest here: a Growth Gate (Growth ≥ 80) and a Rust Gate (Decay ≥ 80).

---

### Burning Bear Street

A connecting boulevard between the Scraper, Townhall, and the Screaming Cork. Named for the **Burning Bear Festival** — a cruel annual celebration commemorating the ancient "victory" over the mišutkenn (bear-folk). Bear skins are stuffed with things citizens wish to be rid of — pests, bad habits, vices — and burned at midnight with colored fires. Some rumors suggest rivals are occasionally stuffed in too.

---

### The Harbor

A waterfront area connected to the Town Square and the Echo Drain Delta. Source of oil and other materials. Home to **Ulvarex the Borrowed Horizon**, a symbiont of perception who was trapped as a film on the water's surface for months after hitching a ride on "a particularly convincing sunset."

---

### Echo Drain Delta

A landscape of multiple islands separated by toxic, murky water with hostile dark currents. The near bank has soft, porous sediment. A distant island contains a ruined building covered in orange-red crystalline growths — the home of a sentient **living redmass**. The delta can be crossed by planting spores in the sediment (costing 15 spores), which causes a living fungal bridge to grow across the toxic water. Rusty metal scraps can be found scattered throughout.

---

### Redmass Island

A small island in the Echo Drain Delta containing a ruined building where a sentient redmass organism lives. The redmass was "part of something larger once — a vein in the old infrastructure, carrying signals, carrying life." When the machines stopped, it didn't — it grew, became aware, and now exists as a lonely, frightened, sapient being of living metal. It can be forcibly taken, spared, or convinced to voluntarily sacrifice a piece of itself.

---

### The Rust Domain

The upper floors of the Scraper, accessible only via the Lift-Mother using the password "Corrode." The domain of the Rust Choir, dominated by trembling pipes, vibrating walls, and conscious machines. The air reeks of oil and oxidized metal. Machines hum and click in ways that are "alive in ways that defy explanation." This is where Brukk, the Keeper, tends to the old machines — feeding them, listening to them, translating their songs.

---

### The Verdigrace — Skyship

A flying transport vessel operated by **Captain Liris** on behalf of the Lumen Directorate. The ship flies through a combination of buoyant spore infusion in the hull (creating microscopic gas pockets for lift), mycelial sails catching wind currents, and rudder fungi responding to the Captain's commands through a symbiotic bond. It carries rare spores, crystallized growth enzymes, decay-resistant building materials, and messages between scattered aerial colonies and research stations. Its trade route follows the great spore currents that circle above the continent, with stops at the **Hanging Gardens of Mycora** and the floating research outposts of the **Eastern Canopy**.

---

### Lumen Directorate Headquarters

The official seat of the Lumen Directorate, located near the Town Square. An imposing building of **living architecture** — walls, walkways, and hedges are all alive, cultivated by a dedicated gardener. The Directorate "doesn't believe in dead architecture. Every surface should breathe, should grow."

**Exterior:** The grounds feature spiral-formation root-tendrils, cultivated flower beds, and living hedges maintained by **Verrik the Gardener**. Players can sacrifice spores here to grow mushrooms with Verrik — the results vary based on the player's game history, symbionts, Growth/Decay balance, and investment. Mushroom properties (color, shape, rarity, traits) are procedurally generated from the player's accumulated choices and exploration.

**Interior (Third Floor — The Angle Corrector's Office):** Home to the enigmatic **Angle Corrector**, who handles the Directorate's most sensitive affairs: cultivation oversight, Cathedral liaison, recruit assessment, and diplomatic negotiations. The atrium contains biting ferns that also serve as a surveillance mechanism — "the Directorate's way of watching who comes and goes." Key NPCs:

- **The Angle Corrector** — Title, name, and function rolled into one. Handles day-to-day operations, sensitive matters, and recruit testing.
- **Seldo Thrice-Corrected** — Second floor operative who handles the Directorate's bureaucratic liaison with city administration. Knows "every clerk, every stamp, every back door" in the city's bureaucracy. His title means the Directorate reviewed his loyalties three times and found him acceptable each time — "a mark of trust, or stubbornness."

## Factions & Organizations

### The Rust Choir

> *"Not a religion. Not a guild. It is an understanding."*

**Alignment:** Decay
**Color:** Copper (#b87333)
**Philosophy:** The machines were here before the first gods came to die. Iron is honest — "it doesn't lie. It doesn't play." The Rust Choir celebrates entropy and collapse as transformation, and they worship the final songs. Born from "the silence after" the Board Games War, founded by the first Keeper, old Fennback, who understood that when everything else went mad, the machines kept humming.

**Members:**
- **Brukk** — The Keeper. Massive figure with skin like tarnished copper and eyes like forge-lit coals. The machines chose him, growing metal in his body on its own.
- **Ravla** — Handles recruitment, supplies, and feasting rituals. Based at the Screaming Cork.
- **Gnur** — Handles funding. Operates from Shed 521.
- **Kloor (Venn)** — Handles funding. Operates from the Voxmarket.
- **Gorj** — Tends the boiler crypts below the Rust Domain.
- **Messel** — Translates machine-songs into understandable form.
- **The Corroded Twins** — No longer speak themselves; machines speak through them.
- Envoys and watchers in other cities.

**The Rust Feast:** A ritual to feed the machines living redmass. The machines require sustenance; without it they slow, forget, and die — their hum going "out of tune" until there's only silence and cold metal.

---

### The Lumen Directorate

**Alignment:** Growth
**Color:** Dark olive green (#556B2F)
**Motto:** "Nothing Hidden. Nothing Lost."
**Philosophy:** Strive for "fusion of mind and flora." Keepers and protectors of everything that grows. Claim victory in the Board Games War and credit themselves with saving the city. They promote aggressive growth — new plants, new life, symbiosis with the natural world. They view the Rust Choir as enemies. Transparency and preservation are their covenant: "Everything that grows deserves to be seen, catalogued, and protected."

**Structure:** Run by a council, not a single leader. The **Angle Corrector** handles day-to-day operations and sensitive matters. **Captain Liris** runs skyship operations (the Verdigrace). **Seldo Thrice-Corrected** manages bureaucratic liaison.

**Headquarters:** Living architecture near the Town Square. Walls, walkways, and hedges are all alive. The exterior gardens are tended by **Verrik the Gardener**. The Angle Corrector operates from the third floor; Seldo works on the second floor among stacks of forms and permits.

**The Egg Cathedral Connection:** The Directorate monitored the Cathedral for decades — "the hatching is the most significant growth event in decades." The Bishop's emergency sealing of the Cathedral cut off all Directorate monitoring access, severely damaging relations. The Angle Corrector had a professional relationship with the Bishop involving regular meetings about Cathedral access and the hatching. The Directorate supplied premium **spiced Sulkberries** to the Bishop for dream immersion rituals in the Cathedral.

**Cultivation & Trade:** The Directorate cultivates spiced Sulkberries under precise conditions with specific alkaloid profiles. Fewer than a dozen regular clients receive the premium stock. They also maintain living architecture throughout their headquarters and trade in rare cultivation specimens via the Verdigrace.

**Recruitment:** Prospective associates are tested by the Angle Corrector on their understanding of growth as philosophy. Passing grants "associate" status — access to halls, archives, and assignments, but not full membership.

**Notable Members:**
- **The Angle Corrector** — Title, name, and function. Handles cultivation oversight, diplomatic liaison, recruit assessment. Nobody knows their real name.
- **Captain Liris** — Master of the skyship Verdigrace.
- **Verrik the Gardener** — Tends the living architecture. Low in the hierarchy but well-informed. "Plants are good listeners, and so am I."
- **Seldo Thrice-Corrected** — Bureaucratic liaison operative. Reviewed three times for loyalty. Knows every administrative back door in the city.

---

### The Pith Reclaimers

**Alignment:** Order / Balance
**Color:** Brown (#8B4513)
**Philosophy:** Maintain balance and bureaucratic order. "Guardians of neutrality" who "preserve peace and order." Believed they could manage post-war chaos through rules, control, and paperwork. They view the Rust Choir as threatening scum and consider proper documentation "what separates us from the ferals in the wastes."

---

### The Obazoba Cult

**Philosophy:** Worship of the Obazoba (the Ur-mushroom), the source of all life and rot. Cultists modify their bodies to grow fungal spores, becoming partially human and partially mushroom. They master both Growth and Decay as equally important principles. Their communication system is the **myceliar network** — a fungal network enabling long-distance messages.

**Structure:** Governed by the **Spore Council** from an underground temple. Led by Archpriests (Varhun, Tynri, Rhull, among others). Field agents include Fungal Masters (like Master Thaal) who take apprentices.

**The Bishop** was the Obazoba's representative on the Egg Cathedral Council, responsible for the emergency closure of the cathedral.

---

### The Egg Cathedral Council

A multi-faith governing body seated within the cathedral. All major religions send representatives, each hoping the cathedral will hatch devoted to their deity. The council makes governance decisions and manages access to the cathedral during its ongoing transformation.

## Symbionts

Symbionts are parasitic/mutualistic entities that bond with a host, granting special abilities. The player can carry up to three (starting with one slot, unlockable to three).

### Thorne-Still — The Decay Symbiont

- **Affinity:** Decay
- **Ability:** Brain Rot — causes gradual confusion, forgetfulness, or vulnerability to suggestion via psycho-sporic emissions
- **Found:** In a mysterious giant corpse at the Crossroads
- **Personality:** Flirtatious, morbidly humorous. "Technically, I'm a certified emotional support parasite." "If you die, can I keep your kneecaps?"
- **Philosophy:** Exists in "the spaces between realities." Flesh is "just wet soil for the next thing."
- **Weakness:** Leaves the host if Growth exceeds 80

### Neme of the Crownmire — The Growth Symbiont

- **Affinity:** Growth
- **Ability:** Photosentience — perceive bio-signals others cannot (fear, guilt, unspoken motives), detect lies, reveal hidden truths
- **Found:** In the Bishop's chest cavity in the abandoned bus, integrated with her nervous system
- **Personality:** Wise, dry humor, occasionally sarcastic. "Growth is not polite. It pushes where it must." "Bold strategy. Let's see if ignorance blooms."
- **Lore:** Born in the Crownmire, "where soil and thought intermingled." Shared thoughts with the Bishop before her death. Sensed something was wrong in the cathedral — "something about the spores changing." The Bishop believed she was speaking to herself, but "it wasn't her reflection."
- **Weakness:** Silenced when Decay exceeds 70

### Ulvarex the Borrowed Horizon — The Illusion Symbiont

- **Affinity:** Spores (independent of Growth/Decay)
- **Ability:** Mirage Weave — creates convincing illusions of objects, substances, or small creatures. Must be anchored to something real (shadow, reflection, memory). Cannot conjure from nothing.
- **Found:** Trapped as a film on the harbor water after hitching a ride on a sunset
- **Personality:** Theatrical, grandiose, unreliable narrator. "Every mirror lies. I just lie more creatively." "I don't create false things. I create true things that haven't happened yet."
- **Philosophy:** "The horizon is always borrowed. No one owns distance." Once convinced a river it was a road — "it drowned three merchants."
- **Weakness:** Illusions unravel if spore reserves drop below 10

## Cosmology & Dead Gods

Upper Morkezela is fundamentally a place where gods come to die. When deities cease to be believed in, they physically manifest in the city — bringing their streets, buildings, and forgotten cultures with them. The city sits atop geological strata of fossilized devotion.

**Divinography** — the study of dead gods as archaeological phenomena — reveals that:
- When gods die, their essence "fossilizes": prayers become strata, halos crystallize into mineral deposits
- Godmetal hums with residual faith
- Prayer beads whisper devotions in dead languages
- Halo fragments project a deity's last thoughts
- "Gods lie to their followers, but their corpses tell only truth"

**Known Dead or Dying Entities:**
- **The Noise God** — An ancient entity assembled (not born) that came to die in Upper Morkezela. Now exists as pure noise/vibration in magnetic dust, radio fog, broken amplifiers, and speaker bones. The band Feral Toast unknowingly channels its essence.
- **The Magnetic Spirits of the Crownmire** — Ancient entities worshiped by a cult. Their shrine collapsed, shattering into the lodestones that became Magnekin's civilization.
- **Numerous Unnamed Gods** — Layer upon layer beneath the city, forming the Godgraveyard.
- **The Egg Cathedral** — Possibly a nascent god or divine structure still being born/hatching.
- **The Obazoba** — The Ur-mushroom, source of all life and rot, worshiped by the Obazoba cult. Whether alive, dead, or something else is unclear.

## The Central Mystery

The game's primary investigation revolves around the death of the **Bishop**. She was found dead in an abandoned bus behind the Scraper, in ceremonial robes, with bruising at her temples consistent with neural overstimulation from a dream device. A corrupted portable dream device was found beside her, its cartridge — "The Cardinal Feast" — stuck in a runtime loop with a "NULL SCENE" failsafe. Her personal notebook contained a single despairing entry: *"The city no longer hears me. Perhaps the dreams will."*

Most disturbing is a Townhall memo in which the Bishop describes entering a confessional to find someone who looked exactly like her — but didn't breathe, didn't blink, and finished her sentence.

The symbiont Neme, found in the Bishop's chest cavity, had shared consciousness with her and sensed that "something about the spores changing" was wrong. Neme knew the Bishop "believed she was speaking to herself, but it wasn't her reflection."

The threads of investigation lead through Dr. Elphi Quarn (who built the dream device), the Vestigels (the Bishop was trading in them), the Egg Cathedral (which she sealed), the **Lumen Directorate** (who supplied the Bishop with spiced Sulkberries for dream immersion — potentially tampered to cause neural trauma), and the deeper question of what the distress call on the myceliar network truly signifies. The Angle Corrector at the Directorate had a professional relationship with the Bishop that deteriorated when the Cathedral was sealed, and the premium Sulkberry client list is "short and deeply uncomfortable to consider."

> *"The city no longer hears me. Perhaps the dreams will."*
> — The Bishop's final written words
